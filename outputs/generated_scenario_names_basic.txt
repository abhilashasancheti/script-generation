<|endoftext|>A new study published today in the journal PLOS One explores the potential use of brain-computer interfaces to help paralyzed patients regain control of their limbs.

In a first-of-its-kind study, a team of scientists has used neurophysiologic measures to determine the optimal electrical signals needed for the movement of prosthetic limbs in a person whose brain is dead but who continues to feel the sensation of using an artificial limb.
